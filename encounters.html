Case #03

Emily Thompson - Postpartum Patient
Descriptors: 32-year-old G3P3, 4 hours after a vaginal birth with a second degree laceration and periurethral tear, no other complications. Last assessment was two hours earlier with the following findings: U/U, firm and midline, lochia rubra moderate, perineum reddened, edematous, incision intact, no drainage or bleeding. Voided once since birth, 300 mL, two hours earlier. Patient breastfed one hour ago.

Listen to Encounter:
Identify Cues
Initial Clinical Decision
[+] REVEAL EXPERT FEEDBACK

The patient should be further evaluated based on having a massive ‘gush’ of lochia or vaginal bleeding, feeling dizzy and lightheaded and her heart racing. Feel a massive ‘gush’ of lochia or other vaginal bleeding may be normal but only in limited circumstances such as when a patient stands or is breastfeeding or during a fundal massage. While a patient may experience dizziness due to orthostatic hypotension, it is not normal for a patient to feel dizzy, lightheaded, or that their heart racing while lying in bed. Bad uterine cramping with breastfeeding is normal for a multiparous patient. Postpartum patients often experience some difficulty urinating and a sore perineum after a vaginal birth. The patient’s last void of 300 mL suggests she was able to empty her bladder fully at that time. Sweating after giving birth is also normal in the first several days postpartum.
